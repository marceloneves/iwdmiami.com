What to Include in Your Project Description

The more we know upfront, the faster we can scope and price. The form's project description field is optional — but if you fill it in, the most useful details:

  1. Florida municipality (city or town) — drives permit timeline and Florida Energy Code scope.
  2. Home age — pre-1978 triggers EPA RRP, pre-1981 may trigger asbestos screening before envelope work.
  3. Square footage (approximate) — drives load calculations and material take-off.
  4. Existing system or condition — what's there now, what's failing, what you want changed.
  5. Timeline — ASAP, within 30 days, 1–3 months, just researching.
  6. Budget range (optional, helps us scope realistically) — under $25K, $25–75K, $75–200K, $200K+.
  7. Any prior estimates received — we will match or beat scope-for-scope, not lowest-bidder play.
  8. Photos or video — attach to a follow-up email or share a Google Drive link in the project description.

Contact FAQs

Florida Contact Questions Answered.

How fast does IWD Miami respond to a contact form?

+
5 minutes during business hours (Mon–Fri 7am–6pm, Sat 8am–2pm). The form goes directly to a Lead General Contractor — not a call center. Off-hours submissions are queued for first thing the next business day. Emergency dispatch is 24/7.

Will I get a quote without an in-person visit?

+
Most quotes require a free on-site visit so we can measure, photograph, and scope cross-trade dependencies properly. For simple service-scope work (faucet replacement, ceiling fan install, single fixture swap), we can sometimes quote remotely from photos and measurements you provide.

Are estimates free in Florida?

+
Yes. Every estimate is free with no commitment. We do an on-site visit, write a line-item estimate, deliver within 24 hours, and you decide. No deposit, no signature required to receive the quote.

What does IWD Miami need from me to start?

+
After you accept the written estimate, we file all Florida Building Code permits under our Florida license, sign the FL home improvement license-compliant contract (max 1/3 deposit per F.S. 489), and book your project on the calendar. The homeowner signs the contract once and pulls no permits.

Do you handle multiple trades on one project?

+
Yes — that is our differential. IWD Miami holds 8 Florida trade licenses in-house: General Contractor, Master Electrician, Master Plumber, Master Gas Fitter, EPA 608 Universal, EPA RRP, Florida Energy HPC, BPI. One project manager, one contract, one warranty.

Can I email IWD Miami instead of using the form?

+
Yes. Email hello@iwdmiami.com directly. Typical reply inside 30 minutes during business hours. Attach photos and a written scope when possible — it accelerates the on-site visit. Email is answered by licensed supervisors, not virtual assistants.

Is IWD Miami a Florida Energy authorized contractor?

+
Yes. IWD Miami is on the active Florida Energy Heat Pump Contractor (HPC) list and Florida Energy Insulation Authorized Contractor list. We file every Florida Energy rebate on your behalf at no extra cost as part of standard scope.

What payment methods does IWD Miami accept?

+
Cash, check, all major credit cards. Florida Energy HEAT Loan (0% APR up to $25,000 over 7 years) for qualifying upgrades. We do not require deposits over the FL home improvement license legal maximum (1/3 of contract value, F.S. 489).
