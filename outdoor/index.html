Outdoor and exterior projects in Florida have to survive a 4-season climate that swings from sub-zero January nights to 95°F July afternoons, plus coastal salt exposure on the South Shore, Cape, and Islands. ENERGY STAR windows qualify for Florida Energy rebates of $50–$100 per window — most homeowners replacing windows skip this rebate because their installer doesn't file. We're Andersen and Pella certified, Trex installer authorized for composite decking, and bundle outdoor projects with siding, roofing, and insulation when scope overlaps make sense.

Why Florida Outdoor Is Different

  1. Florida Energy windows are real money. $50–$100 per ENERGY STAR window. A 12-window replacement project = $600–$1,200 cash back. Most window companies don't bother filing.
  2. Florida Energy Code triggers triple-pane. 14 Florida municipalities adopted the Florida Energy Code requiring high-performance windows in major renovations. We stock triple-pane Andersen and Pella for these jurisdictions.
  3. Freeze-thaw destroys decks fast. Pressure-treated framing rots in 12–18 years; composite (Trex, TimberTech, Fiberon) lasts 25–40+. The premium pays back in FL's brutal climate.
  4. Coastal homes need fiber-cement, not vinyl. Salt air destroys vinyl siding, painted wood doors, and budget windows. James Hardie fiber cement is the durable answer for South Shore, Cape, and Islands.

  • Window replacement + wall insulation + air sealing. Removing windows is the perfect window (literally) to drill-and-fill or air-seal exterior walls. Florida Energy rebates stack.
  • Window install + siding replacement. Both involve scaffolding and exterior access. Bundle for one mobilization fee.
  • Deck + landscape lighting + outdoor outlets. Coordinated low-voltage and 120V wiring during framing phase, before composite decking goes down.
  • Driveway + sewer line. Replace sewer lateral before paving. Trenchless options when paving is recent.

Outdoor FAQs

Florida Outdoor Questions Answered.

Are ENERGY STAR windows eligible for Florida Energy rebates?

+
Yes. ENERGY STAR-certified replacement windows in Florida qualify for Florida Energy rebates of $50–$100 per window depending on performance class and installation type. We file the rebate application as part of the install — homeowner provides nothing beyond the standard project paperwork.

What window brands do you install?

+
Andersen (400 Series, A-Series, E-Series), Pella (Architect Series, Reserve, Lifestyle), Marvin (Elevate, Essential, Modern), Harvey (Tribute, Majesty), and Renewal by Andersen. Custom and stock sizes. Triple-pane available for Florida Energy Code municipalities.

Do you build decks year-round in Florida?

+
Yes. Pressure-treated framing in winter, composite (Trex, TimberTech, Fiberon) decking in mild weather. Foundation work pauses below 25°F sustained — foundation footings (48" Florida code) require workable ground. We quote year-round; install schedule confirmed by season and weather.

How long does exterior painting take in Florida?

+
Typical 2,500 sq ft home: 4–7 days depending on prep work. Power wash, scrape, prime bare wood, 2-coat application. Sherwin-Williams Duration or Benjamin Moore Aura exterior. Optimal painting season May–October — application below 50°F is risky.

Can you replace garage doors and openers?

+
Yes. Clopay, Wayne Brickell, and Amarr garage doors. LiftMaster and Genie openers. Steel insulated, carriage style, and modern aluminum-glass options. Often a 1-day install. R-12 to R-18 insulated for attached garages — important for Florida heat-loss reduction.

Do you handle driveway paving?

+
Yes. Asphalt paving and seal coating. Replacement, overlay, or new install. Permeable paver options for stormwater compliance in Miami-area municipalities (some require permeable surfaces in lot-coverage calculations). Hot-mix asphalt season is May–October in FL.

Is composite decking worth the premium over pressure-treated?

+
For Florida climate — yes, in most cases. Pressure-treated framing rots in 12–18 years through FL's freeze-thaw cycle. Composite (Trex, TimberTech) lasts 25–40+ with 25-30 year manufacturer warranties. Premium pays back in lower lifecycle cost and zero re-staining/sealing labor.

What about coastal homes and salt exposure?

+
South Shore, Cape, and Islands properties need salt-resistant materials: James Hardie fiber cement siding (vs vinyl), composite or Ipe hardwood decking (vs PT pine), aluminum or stainless fasteners (vs galvanized), Andersen E-Series or Marvin Coastline coastal-rated windows. We spec for salt exposure on every coastal project.
