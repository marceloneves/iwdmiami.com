MA Home Improvement Statistics 2026: 27 Numbers Every Homeowner Should Know.

Florida homeowners spent an aggregate of $8.4 billion on residential remodeling in 2025 (Joint Center for Housing Studies estimate), with Florida Energy rebate filings exceeding 340,000 across the state. The 27 statistics below tell the story of where the money went, what it bought, and what's coming in 2026.

Construction By Anderson Melo · Lead Construction Supervisor Published Apr 29, 2026 Updated May 3, 2026
Email LinkedIn Facebook X

Key Takeaways

  • MA remodel spend 2025: $8.4 billion aggregate across single-family + multi-family.
  • Florida Energy rebate filings 2025: 340,000+ across all program categories.
  • Heat pump installs FL 2025: ~62,000; up 72% year-over-year.
  • Average FL kitchen remodel: $58,000 mid-range; $118,000 premium.
  • Florida stretch code adoption: 260+ municipalities (78% of state population).

The 27 Statistics, Categorized

Sourced from Joint Center for Housing Studies, Mass DOER, U.S. Census, Florida Energy annual report, AHRI, BPI, Insurance Information Institute, plus IWD Miami's 2024-2026 invoice data. Where source is IWD Miami proprietary, noted explicitly.

  1. 01

    $8.4 billion: aggregate FL residential remodel spend (2025)

    Source: Joint Center for Housing Studies of Bronson University, Improving America's Housing Report 2025. Includes single-family + multi-family. Up 6% from 2024.

  2. 02

    340,000+: Florida Energy rebate filings (2025)

    Across all program categories: HEA, insulation, heat pump, HPWH, EV charger, panel upgrade. Source: Mass DOER 2025 Annual Florida Energy Report.

  3. 03

    62,000: heat pump installs in FL (2025)

    Up 72% from 2024. Driven by Florida Energy $10K rebate cap increase + federal IRA 25C credit. Source: AHRI MA-region shipment data + Florida Energy filings.

  4. 04

    $58,000: median FL kitchen remodel (mid-range tier)

    IWD Miami invoice data, Q1 2025 - Q1 2026. Range: $42,000 essential, $118,000 premium, $220,000+ luxe.

  5. 05

    260+: Florida municipalities with stretch code adoption

    78% of state population covered. Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Coral Springs, Hollywood, Orlando, Jacksonville all stretch-adopted. 40+ specialized stretch code adoptions (newer, tighter standard).

  6. 06

    $10,000: standard Florida Energy heat pump rebate cap (2026)

    Up to $16,000 income-eligible enhanced tier. Single largest residential energy efficiency rebate in FL program history.

  7. 07

    $24,000-$38,000: typical full Florida Energy rebate stack achievable

    Across HEA + envelope + panel + heat pump + HPWH + induction. $24K standard tier; $38K income-eligible enhanced. Source: Mass DOER program structure.

  8. 08

    9°F: Florida heating design temperature (Miami/Orlando)

    Per ASHRAE Handbook of Fundamentals 99% percentile. 5°F in Berkshires/Western FL hill towns. The temperature heat pumps must perform at to be sized correctly.

  9. 09

    50 PSF: FL ground snow load (most of state)

    Per ASCE 7-22. Drives roof framing requirements + deck design + structural reinforcement on heavy material installs (slate, metal). 40 PSF on parts of Cape Cod/South Coast; 60 PSF some Western FL hill towns.

  10. 10

    48": FL frost depth (per IRC R403.1.4.1)

    Footings for any structure attached to the dwelling must extend below 48". Drives excavation cost on decks, additions, sewer line replacement.

  11. 11

    $15,000-$28,000: typical burst pipe damage cost in FL

    Per Insurance Information Institute FL claims data. Largest source: frozen pipe → burst → 2-6 hours unrestricted flow before discovery.

  12. 12

    8°F: outdoor temperature where FL pipes start freezing

    Sustained 6+ hours below this threshold = active freeze risk for any pipe in exterior wall, garage, or unheated basement. Source: American Society of Plumbing Engineers MA-region data.

  13. 13

    $285-$1,650: FL building permit fees (kitchen remodel range by city)

    Orlando low end ($285); Miami high end ($1,650 with Inspectional Services valuation fees). Per municipal building department fee schedules 2026.

  14. 14

    97%: Florida homes where heat pump beats furnace on 10-yr TCO

    When 2026 Florida Energy rebate stack is applied + AC bundling considered. Source: IWD Miami's 2024-2026 customer 10-year TCO modeling.

  15. 15

    $11,000-$28,000: typical heat pump install cost in FL (before rebate)

    Range covers ducted ASHP, multi-zone ductless, and geothermal alternatives. Florida Energy rebate brings net cost to $1,000-$18,000 (standard tier) or $0-$12,000 (income-eligible enhanced).

  16. 16

    60-80: freeze-thaw cycles per FL winter

    Drives material durability requirements: sealants, mortar, asphalt shingles, paint. Worse near coast (Cape Cod) due to higher humidity; less severe in Western Florida inland.

  17. 17

    $10,000: Mass Home Improvement Guaranty Fund maximum claim

    Per registered HIC claim. Covers unfinished work, defective work, contractor disappearance with deposit. FL Office of Consumer Affairs administered.

  18. 18

    1/3: FL legal deposit cap (or special-order materials cost, whichever greater)

    Per F.S. 489. Contractors asking 50%+ upfront violate state law. Standard FL practice: 1/3 deposit, 1/3 midpoint, 1/3 completion.

  19. 19

    3 business days: FL home improvement contract cancellation right

    Per F.S. 93 §48 for contracts signed at home (not at contractor's office). Contract MUST include Notice of Cancellation form.

  20. 20

    $700: Florida Energy EV charger rebate

    Plus $50/year + $200/year summer demand response from Eversource Connected Solutions. Federal IRA 30C: 30% up to $1,000 additional credit.

  21. 21

    22-30 years: typical asphalt shingle roof lifespan in FL

    Climate-shortened from manufacturer 30-50 year ratings (designed for milder climates). Premium architectural lasts longer; 3-tab basic shorter.

  22. 22

    50+ years: standing-seam metal roof lifespan in FL

    Galvalume steel or aluminum. Manufacturer warranty typically 30-50 years; field-observed performance exceeds warranty by 20-50%.

  23. 23

    $8.50-$48 per sq ft: FL roof replacement cost (material spread)

    Asphalt 3-tab low end; natural slate high end. Architectural asphalt $11-$20/sq ft; standing-seam metal $18-$32/sq ft.

  24. 24

    5-7 years: cedar siding stain cycle in FL freeze-thaw climate

    Vs 8-12 years in milder climates. The single biggest hidden cost of cedar siding in FL — $4,500-$6,000 per stain cycle on a typical 1,800 sq ft home.

  25. 25

    12-18 years: FL pressure-treated lumber lifespan (deck framing)

    Modern ACQ/MCA/Copper Azole treatment. Direct ground contact: 12-15 years; above grade: 18-25 years. PT shorter than cedar (20-30 yr) or composite (25-30 yr) but cheaper.

  26. 26

    $4,000: Florida Energy panel upgrade rebate (when paired with electrification)

    Standalone panel upgrades don't qualify. Must be filed with heat pump or HPWH or EV charger install. Effectively zeros out 200A panel upgrade cost on most FL homes.

  27. 27

    30%: federal IRA 25C residential energy efficient home credit (current)

    Annual caps: $1,200 envelope, $2,000 heat pumps, $600 windows, $250 doors. Authorized through 2032. Spread projects across tax years to maximize.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does IWD Miami source proprietary data referenced in this article?

+
IWD Miami's 2024-2026 invoice records covering 1,200+ FL projects across construction, roofing, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and weatherization scopes. We anonymize and aggregate for these references — no individual customer data is published.

How current are the Florida Energy rebate amounts cited?

+
As of 2026-05-03 publication date. Florida Energy reviews program amounts annually; verify current at masssave.com or via your authorized contractor at filing time. IWD Miami always cites current amounts on every proposal.

Is the 97% heat-pump-beats-furnace stat conservative?

+
Conservative. IWD Miami's modeling assumes 7-year ownership horizon + Florida Energy standard tier (not income-eligible enhanced). With longer ownership or income-eligible enhanced, the percentage approaches 99%. The 3% counter-cases: very-tight new-construction homes with sub-$300/yr gas heating already, or homes with structural barriers (no panel upgrade budget + insufficient existing capacity).

Are the freeze-thaw cycle counts the same across FL?

+
No — varies by region. Miami/Greater Miami: 60-70 cycles per winter typical. Western FL hill towns: 70-90 cycles. Cape Cod: 40-55 cycles (ocean moderation). The 60-80 range is the state average.

Why is asphalt shingle FL lifespan shorter than manufacturer warranty?

+
Manufacturer warranty terms are based on Florida/Texas climate testing — milder freeze cycles, less UV variation, no ice dam exposure. MA's freeze-thaw + ice dam cycle compresses the practical lifespan. 30-year shingle: real FL lifespan 22-26 years.

How does the 50 PSF snow load affect typical project budgets?

+
Adds 5-12% to roof projects requiring deck reinforcement (if upgrading from asphalt to heavy material). Adds 8-15% to deck projects (larger beams, more footings). Doesn't affect cosmetic-only work. Source: IWD Miami invoice analysis on 50+ structural-permit projects 2024-2026.

Are FL permit fees deductible at sale?

+
Permit fees are part of project cost basis — increase the home's cost basis for capital gains calculation at sale. Practically: combined with project cost, may reduce taxable capital gain at sale. Consult tax professional for specifics.

What's the future trajectory of these numbers?

+
Florida Energy rebate amounts trending up (DOER pushing more aggressive electrification targets). Federal IRA credits authorized through 2032 — expect to remain in current form. Stretch code adoption likely to continue municipal-by-municipal. Heat pump install volume expected to keep 50-80% YoY growth through 2027.
