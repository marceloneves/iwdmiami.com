Interior specialty trades in Florida are 80% of what you actually see and touch in a renovation — yet most contractors treat them as afterthoughts. Hardwood floor sanding swirl marks, tile cut-in to baseboards that's wavy, paint roller marks on flat ceilings — these are visible every day for the next 20 years. Miami housing predates 1978 in most of the urban core, which means EPA RRP lead-safe procedures on every wall sand, baseboard prep, or finish-carpentry disturbance. We hold the Schluter-certified installer credential for waterproof tile assemblies, EPA RRP for lead-safe painting, and bring detail-oriented finish carpenters with 10+ years on tools.

Why Interior Finish Work Is Different

  1. Pre-1978 housing means lead. Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Dunnellon, and most older FL towns have lead paint. EPA RRP-certified containment is required for any sanding, scraping, or cutting that disturbs painted surfaces. Generic handymen aren't certified.
  2. Tile in wet areas needs Schluter. Florida bathrooms freeze-thaw seasonally even with heat. Without proper waterproofing (Schluter Kerdi membrane, Ditra uncoupling), tile fails within 5–10 years. We're Schluter-certified.
  3. Hardwood in FL needs proper acclimation. Wide humidity swings (15% RH winter to 70% RH summer) cup and gap hardwood that wasn't properly acclimated and sanded. Dustless sanding equipment + 14-day acclimation make the difference.
  4. Interior paint cut-in is judged daily. A line that's 1/8" wavy at the ceiling reads as cheap forever. We don't subcontract — our painter does the cut-in by hand.

Specialty FAQs

Florida Interior Questions Answered.

Do you refinish hardwood floors in Florida?

+
Yes. Sanding, staining, and finishing of existing hardwood floors. Dustless sanding equipment (vacuum-attached belt sanders + edgers). Water-based and oil-based polyurethane. Typical 1,000 sq ft refinish: 3–4 days from start to walkable surface.

What flooring materials do you install?

+
Hardwood (red oak, white oak, hickory, maple), engineered hardwood, luxury vinyl plank (LVP), porcelain and ceramic tile, natural stone, carpet. Solid hardwood acclimated 14 days to Florida humidity before install — critical to prevent cupping and gapping in FL's seasonal RH swings.

Are interior painting estimates free?

+
Yes. Free in-home estimate covering wall prep (drywall repair, sanding, priming), trim work, ceiling, and 2-coat application. Sherwin-Williams Emerald or Benjamin Moore Aura as standard finishes. EPA RRP lead-safe procedures on pre-1978 homes (most of Miami's housing stock).

Do you handle handyman services or only large projects?

+
Both. Handyman services for small repairs, mounting, fixture replacement, door adjustment — 2-hour booking minimum, no trip charge. Larger remodels coordinated through our Construction silo. Anything requiring a Florida trade license (electrical, plumbing, gas, HVAC) routes to the appropriate licensed trade in-house.

Can you do tile installation including showers?

+
Yes. Backsplash, bathroom walls, shower surrounds, floors. Porcelain, ceramic, glass mosaic, natural stone (marble, travertine, slate). Schluter Kerdi waterproofing system for wet areas — the gold standard for tile shower assemblies. Ditra uncoupling membrane for floors over wood substrate.

Do you handle junk removal?

+
Yes. Construction debris, appliance removal, furniture and bulk junk. Often bundled with renovation projects to clear the work area before crews arrive. Florida DEP-compliant disposal — we sort recyclables, route metals to scrap, and dispose only what can't be recycled.

How long should hardwood floors acclimate before installation?

+
In Florida: minimum 14 days in the install location at the home's normal seasonal humidity (typically 35–50% RH). Skipping acclimation = cupping in summer (high humidity) or gapping in winter (low humidity). It's the #1 cause of hardwood callbacks. We don't compromise on this.

What about EPA RRP for pre-1978 homes?

+
Federal EPA RRP rule requires lead-safe certified containment for any work disturbing painted surfaces in pre-1978 homes. Most Miami-area housing predates 1978. We're EPA RRP certified and follow lead-safe protocols on every applicable project — plastic containment, HEPA vacuum cleanup, tested clearance before reoccupancy.

Floors? Tile? Paint?

Free in-home measure. EPA RRP lead-safe. Schluter-certified waterproofing. 5-minute response.
