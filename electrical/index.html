Electrical in Florida is no longer about replacing fuses. The Commonwealth is electrifying — heat pumps, EV chargers, induction ranges, heat pump water heaters, smart home loads — and most pre-1990 Florida homes were wired for 100-amp service when the average home pulled 30 amps at peak. Today's electrified home routinely needs 200 amps. We hold the Florida Master Electrician license required to pull permits, are MOR-EV authorized for EV charger rebate filing, and run a 24/7 emergency dispatch for storm damage, panel failure, and electrical fires. Knob-and-tube removal in pre-1940 Miami homes is a routine specialty.

Why Florida Electrical Is Different

  1. Electrification stacks loads fast. Heat pump (40–60A) + EV charger (50A) + induction range (40A) + tankless water heater (80A peak) = 210+ amps before lights and outlets. Most older FL homes need a 200-amp panel upgrade just to enable basic electrification.
  2. MOR-EV rebate program. Florida MOR-EV provides rebates for residential EV charger installation when paired with eligible EV purchase. We are MOR-EV authorized — we file the rebate as part of the install.
  3. Knob-and-tube is unsafe + non-insurable. Most pre-1940 Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Dunnellon housing has knob-and-tube in walls. Florida code does not allow new circuits to be added to K&T. Many home insurers refuse to insure homes with active K&T. We replace it during any major electrical upgrade.
  4. Florida Energy income-eligible panel upgrade rebate. Income-eligible homeowners frequently qualify for free panel upgrades when paired with electrification.

The 5 Pillar Electrical Services

  • 200-Amp Panel Upgrade

    Electrification Prereq$3,500 – $6,500

    The single most common upgrade in pre-1990 FL homes. New 200-amp service drop coordination with utility, new meter socket, new load center (Square D, Eaton, Siemens), service mast and grounding to current code. Required before most heat pump or EV charger installs.

  • EV Charger Installation

    MOR-EV RebateTesla, ChargePoint, Wallbox

    Tesla Wall Connector, ChargePoint Home Flex, Wallbox Pulsar, JuiceBox, Grizzl-E. Hardwired or NEMA 14-50 outlet. Load calculation to confirm panel headroom. MOR-EV rebate filed as part of the install — we handle the paperwork.

  • Generator Installation

    Whole-Home BackupGenerac, Kohler, Briggs

    Standby generators (10kW–26kW) with whole-home transfer switches. Natural gas or propane fuel. Generac and Kohler authorized dealer/installer. Often bundled with panel upgrade. Critical for Florida climate storm outages.

  • Knob-and-Tube Removal

    Insurance RequiredPre-1940 Miami specialty

    Most home insurers refuse to insure homes with active knob-and-tube. We remove K&T in walls, run new Romex or BX, restore drywall and plaster. Often coordinated with insulation upgrade since K&T cannot be insulated over.

  • 24/7 Emergency Electrician

    Storm + Panel Failure5-min Greater Miami

    Electrical fires, panel failure, sparking outlets, storm-damage service drops. 5-minute Master Electrician dispatch within Greater Miami. Same-day for outer Florida. We coordinate utility for service-drop replacement after fallen-tree damage.

Electrical FAQs

Florida Electrical Questions Answered.

Why do I need a 200-amp panel upgrade for a heat pump or EV charger?

+
Most Florida homes built before 1990 have 100-amp service already near capacity. Adding a heat pump (40–60 amps) or EV charger (50 amps) requires headroom — a 200-amp panel upgrade gives the capacity and resets the home's electrical baseline. Adding an induction range or tankless water heater also requires the upgrade.

Are MOR-EV rebates available for EV charger installation?

+
Yes. The Florida MOR-EV program provides rebates for residential EV charger installation when paired with an eligible EV purchase. We are MOR-EV authorized and file the rebate application as part of the install — homeowner provides EV purchase documentation, we handle the rest.

Do you install Tesla Wall Connectors?

+
Yes. Tesla Wall Connector (current generation), Tesla Universal Wall Connector (J1772 + NACS), ChargePoint Home Flex, Wallbox Pulsar, and other major Level 2 chargers. Hardwired or NEMA 14-50 outlet install. We also do load calculations for homes near service capacity to determine if a panel upgrade is needed first.

Can you replace knob-and-tube wiring in older Florida homes?

+
Yes. Florida code does not allow new circuits to be added to knob-and-tube. We replace knob-and-tube alongside any major electrical upgrade and coordinate with insulation contractors when walls are open — K&T cannot be insulated over per code. Most home insurers refuse to insure homes with active K&T.

What generators do you install?

+
Generac, Kohler, and Briggs & Stratton standby generators (10kW–26kW). Whole-home transfer switches. Natural gas or propane fuel. Often bundled with panel upgrade for older homes. Critical for Florida climate storm outages — average FL home loses power 1–2 times per year.

Do you provide 24/7 emergency electrician service?

+
Yes. Electrical fires, panel failures, sparking outlets, and storm-damage service drops dispatched 24/7 in Greater Miami. 5-minute Master Electrician response. Same-day for outer Florida. We coordinate utility company for service-drop replacement after fallen-tree damage.

Is income-eligible Florida Energy panel upgrade really free?

+
For income-eligible Florida homeowners (under 60% of state median income) when paired with electrification (heat pump, etc.) — often yes, 100% covered under Florida Energy's enhanced income-eligible programs. We verify eligibility during the Home Energy Assessment and coordinate the panel upgrade with HVAC scope.

How long does a 200-amp panel upgrade take?

+
Typical 200-amp panel upgrade: 1–2 days. Includes utility coordination for service-drop replacement (4-hour power outage during the swap), new meter socket, new load center, all new circuits transferred and labeled, grounding to current code. Permit pulled and inspected.
