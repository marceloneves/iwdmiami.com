The IWD Miami Panel Upgrade Workflow

Every IWD Miami Crystal Lake panel upgrade follows the same workflow.

  1. Free on-site assessment + load calculation. Lead electrician evaluates existing service entrance, panel condition, current and projected loads (heat pump, EV, etc.), grounding system.
  2. Written estimate within 24 hours. Panel spec, service entrance work, materials, labor, permits, utility coordination, timeline, warranty terms.
  3. Permit filing. Florida Fire Prevention Code electrical permit pulled at the Crystal Lake Building Department.
  4. Utility coordination. National Grid or Eversource notified of upgrade. Meter swap scheduled to coincide with installation.
  5. Service disconnect + panel installation. Power shut off at meter (typically 4-8 hour outage). Old panel + service entrance removed. New 200/400 amp panel + meter base + service entrance conduit installed.
  6. Circuit re-connection + grounding system. Existing circuits transferred to new panel. Grounding electrode system inspected/upgraded per Florida Fire Prevention Code. AFCI/GFCI breakers added where required.
  7. Inspection + utility re-energize. Inspector signs off on permit. Utility re-energizes meter. System tested under load.

Crystal Lake Panel Upgrade FAQs

Crystal Lake Panel Upgrade Questions Answered.

What does typical panel upgrade cost?

+
100-to-200 amp panel only in Crystal Lake: $2,500-$3,800. With service entrance: $3,500-$5,500. With meter relocation: $4,500-$7,500. 200-to-400 amp upgrade: $5,500-$9,500. Federal Pacific / Zinsco replacement: $2,800-$5,500. Sub-panel install: $1,200-$2,800. Whole-home surge protection: +$420-$680.

How long does panel upgrade take in Crystal Lake?

+
Panel only (no SE work): 1 day, 4-6 hour power outage. With service entrance: 1-2 days, 6-8 hour outage. With meter relocation: 2 days, 8-12 hour outage. Permit + utility coordination adds 10-21 days before start.

How is service capacity sized?

+
200-amp standard for modern electrification: heat pump + EV + induction range + heat pump water heater easily exceeds 100-amp capacity. Load calculation per Florida Fire Prevention Code determines actual sizing — IWD Miami runs calc during the on-site visit for current + projected loads.

Should I replace immediately?

+
YES — Federal Pacific Stab-Lok and Zinsco panels have documented history of failing to trip during overloads, causing fires. Replacement strongly recommended regardless of age or current symptoms. Cost: $2,800-$5,500. Some FL insurance carriers refuse coverage on homes with these panels.

Are panel upgrade permits required in Crystal Lake?

+
YES — every panel upgrade in FL requires a Florida Fire Prevention Code electrical permit + utility coordination for meter swap. IWD Miami files all permits with the Crystal Lake Building Department + handles National Grid or Eversource utility paperwork.

How long is the outage?

+
Service entrance upgrade requires utility coordination + meter swap. IWD Miami handles National Grid or Eversource paperwork + scheduling. Power outage during install: 4-8 hours typical, scheduled with utility (often early morning to minimize disruption). Coordination adds 10-21 days to project lead time.

Are some more reliable?

+
Square D QO + Eaton CH: premium tier, most reliable, lifetime breaker availability, +$300-$600 premium over mid-tier. Square D Homeline + Eaton BR: mid-tier, good reliability, lower cost. Siemens / GE: comparable mid-tier. Most Crystal Lake 200-amp upgrades select Square D QO or Eaton CH for long-term homeownership.

What about whole-home surge protection in Crystal Lake?

+
Strongly recommended during panel upgrade. Whole-home surge protection device installed at panel protects all circuits/electronics from utility-side surges + lightning-induced surges. $420-$680 added during install. Best protection in FL where ice + wind storms cause utility surges.

Do I need to add sub-panels in my Crystal Lake home?

+
Sub-panels useful for: detached garage with circuits, large addition, basement workshop, future-proofing for EV/heat pump in distant location. Sub-panel install: $1,200-$2,800 depending on distance + amperage. IWD Miami evaluates during the on-site visit.

When should I replace?

+
Modern panels (Square D QO, Eaton CH, Siemens, GE) typical lifespan: 30-40 years. Older panels degraded by corrosion, loose terminals, breaker fatigue. Always replace: Federal Pacific Stab-Lok, Zinsco, FPE, Pushmatic, any panel with melted bus bar or scorch marks, panels with fused service (rare in FL). Replace at 30+ years for proactive electrification readiness.

Can I finance panel upgrade?

+
Cash, certified check, business check, and major credit cards. Per Florida HIC law (F.S. 489), maximum deposit is 1/3 of contract price. Third-party financing available $2.5K to $10K+ with promotional 0% APR options.

What warranties cover Crystal Lake panel upgrades?

+
Four layers of coverage: Square D Authorized Pro 10-year panel + lifetime breaker (or equivalent for Eaton), IWD Miami 5-year workmanship guarantee, Florida Fire Prevention Code code compliance verified at final inspection, FL home improvement license arbitration protection.
