Hillsborough County FAQs

Working in Hillsborough County Common Questions.

What credentials are required for residential contractors in Hillsborough County?

+
Throughout Hillsborough County, home builders have to hold FL HIC registration (statute) and a Certified General Contractor (CGC) for any framing work. Specialized crafts like plumbing demand separate Commonwealth licenses.

How long do permits take in Hillsborough County?

+
Typical home permit timeline within Hillsborough County varies from 5 to 14 working days for standard scopes. Larger jobs needing framing sign-off may extend 3 to 6 weeks. IWD Miami submit all application under our license — the homeowner signs nothing.

Do conservation overlays govern work in Hillsborough County?

+
Absolutely — many cities in Hillsborough County administer heritage overlay review bodies that have to review envelope work. That governs siding choices, window types, and color specifications.

Which hurricane wind design rating applies in Hillsborough County?

+
Structures throughout Hillsborough County must carry load specifications of 30-40 mph design wind speed per current Florida Building Code requirements. That figure determines sheathing thickness, truss spec, and barrier installation requirements.

What types of work does IWD Miami handle in Hillsborough County?

+
IWD Miami delivers eight trade silos in Hillsborough County: Construction & Remodeling, Roofing & Siding, HVAC & Heating, Plumbing, Electrical, Energy & Insulation, Outdoor & Exterior, and Specialty & Interior work. Every trade is directly credentialed — never third-party handoffs for Hillsborough County owners.

Are emergency repairs available in Hillsborough County?

+
Yes — and on weekends too. IWD Miami covers 24/7 emergency response for storm damage across Hillsborough County. Same-day dispatch delivers under 4 hours for Hillsborough County addresses during high-demand seasons.

Will IWD Miami work with my insurance company on a Hillsborough County claim?

+
Yes. Our team catalog damage, provide line-item estimates, and liaise with Hillsborough County owner adjuster reps from day-one contact through settlement.

Can I finance my Hillsborough County project?

+
Yes — multiple options. IWD Miami provides third-party financing for Hillsborough County engagements ranging from $2,500 to $100,000+, with cost-of-capital options from 0% promotional to market long-term structures. Application happens in under 60 seconds through our financing tool.

What warranties cover IWD Miami work in Hillsborough County?

+
Every IWD Miami Hillsborough County project comes with four forms of protection: manufacturer warranty (up to 50 years registered on the homeowner behalf), IWD Miami 5-year workmanship guarantee, Florida Building Code code-compliance verification at final inspection, and Florida HIC arbitration protection per F.S. 489.

Is cross-trade coordination available in Hillsborough County?

+
Always. IWD Miami holds each required Florida trade license in-house: CGC, HIC, Master Electrician, Master Plumber, Master Gas Fitter, EPA RRP. Hillsborough County clients enjoy unified project manager spanning each trade line — eliminating out-of-house coordination gaps.
