The questions Florida homeowners actually ask before hiring a contractor — answered straight, no upsell.

What home services does IWD Miami offer in Florida?

+
IWD Miami is a licensed multi-trade contractor offering eight categories of work: construction & remodeling (kitchens, baths, additions, ADUs, whole-home renovations), roofing & siding, HVAC & heating (heat pumps, boilers, furnaces, mini-splits), plumbing (repair, drains, sumps, gas lines, 24/7 emergency), electrical (panel upgrades, EV chargers, generators), weatherization & insulation, outdoor & exterior (decks, windows, doors), and specialty interior (flooring, painting, tile). All trades coordinated under one Florida contractor license.

Do you handle historic district permits in Miami?

+
Yes. We routinely pass reviews at the Miami Landmarks Commission, Brickell Architectural Commission, Brickell Architectural Commission, South End Landmark District, and similar bodies in Fort Lauderdale and Desoto Lakes. We handle every permit application, historic commission filing, and pre-approval material list. Most of our Brickell and Brickell projects pass first review.

Why hire a multi-trade contractor instead of separate specialists?

+
A typical Florida renovation involves five to seven trades (demolition, construction, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, finish work, exterior). Hiring separately means the homeowner becomes the project manager — coordinating schedules, resolving trade conflicts, chasing permits, and dealing with each contractor's separate warranty. We deliver every trade in-house with a single project manager, single warranty, single point of accountability. The same crew that opens the wall closes it. See how our process works →

What is the Florida Florida Energy Code and how does it affect my project?

+
The Florida Florida Energy Code is the building energy code adopted by 300+ Florida municipalities (including Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Coral Springs, Hollywood, Dunnellon). It requires higher energy efficiency standards than baseline code, especially in new construction and major renovations — affecting insulation R-values, window U-factors, air sealing requirements, and HVAC sizing. The newer Florida Energy Code goes further and effectively requires all-electric heating in new builds. We design every IWD Miami project to comply with whichever code applies in your municipality, and pair retrofits with Florida Energy rebates to offset the cost.
