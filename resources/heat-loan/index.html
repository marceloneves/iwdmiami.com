What the Florida Energy HEAT Loan Actually Is

The Florida Energy HEAT Loan is a 0% APR unsecured loan that finances energy efficiency upgrades on Florida residential properties. "HEAT" stands for Home Energy Assistance Team. The loan is funded by participating Florida banks and credit unions, with Florida Energy buying down the interest to 0% on the homeowner's behalf.

It is not a IWD Miami product, not a Florida Energy program direct loan, not a HELOC. It is a third-party loan with subsidized interest. The homeowner makes monthly payments to the participating lender, exactly like any other personal loan — except the rate is 0%.

Terms and Limits

Interest rate
0% APR for the entire loan term. No rate-reset, no escalation.
Maximum amount
$25,000 per project. Higher amounts in some scenarios for income-eligible homeowners or whole-home electrification packages.
Term
Up to 7 years. Most homeowners select 5–7 years to minimize monthly payment.
Origination fees
None. The HEAT Loan has no application fee, no origination fee, no prepayment penalty.
Eligible upgrades
Heat pumps, insulation, air sealing, heat pump water heaters, ENERGY STAR windows, high-efficiency boilers (in some cases), solar paired with electrification, panel upgrades enabling electrification.
Eligible properties
1–4 unit Florida residential properties. Must be the homeowner's primary residence or a long-term rental.

How HEAT Loan Stacks with Florida Energy Rebate and Federal Credits

The HEAT Loan is designed to be stacked. A typical Florida heat pump install:

  • Investment before rebate: $24,800
  • Florida Energy rebate: –$9,400 (filed by IWD Miami, posts 4–8 weeks after install)
  • HEAT Loan financing: $15,400 at 0% APR over 7 years = $183/month
  • Federal 25C credit: –$2,000 (claimed on your next federal tax return via IRS Form 5695)
  • Out-of-pocket at install: $0

Most homeowners pay the loan off ahead of schedule with the Florida Energy rebate check and the next-year federal tax credit refund. The point is: the financing exists so you do not write a check at install.

How to Apply

IWD Miami files the HEAT Loan paperwork as part of the standard rebate-eligible project scope. Sequence:

  1. Sign the IWD Miami contract. We submit the project details to a participating Florida Energy HEAT Loan lender (typically a Florida credit union).
  2. The lender pulls a soft credit check. Approval is typically 1–3 business days. Underwriting requires a Florida Energy program-eligible scope and a Florida residential property.
  3. You sign the loan documents. Funds disburse direct to the contractor (IWD Miami) at install milestones, or as a single payment at project completion depending on the lender.
  4. Monthly payments begin 30 days after disbursement. You can pay off the loan at any time with no penalty — most homeowners use the Florida Energy rebate check and federal credit refund to accelerate payoff.

What the HEAT Loan Will Not Do

  • Not for non-eligible projects. If the underlying project is not Florida Energy program-eligible (kitchen remodel, addition, deck), HEAT Loan financing is not available.
  • Not a credit-rebuild tool. Underwriting is real — typical approval threshold is mid-600s FICO. Income verification required.
  • Not a substitute for the rebate. The HEAT Loan finances what you owe after the rebate; it does not replace the rebate.
  • Not stackable across non-eligible scope. If you bundle heat pump install with a kitchen remodel, only the heat pump portion is HEAT Loan-eligible.

The Resource FAQs

Florida The Resource Questions Answered.

Is the HEAT Loan really 0% APR?

+
Yes. The interest is bought down to 0% by Florida Energy on the homeowner's behalf. The lender (a participating FL bank or credit union) extends the loan at the subsidized rate. There are no hidden fees, no escalation clauses.

Can I use the HEAT Loan for a project IWD Miami did not sell me?

+
Yes — the HEAT Loan works with any Florida Energy authorized contractor in Florida. IWD Miami is on the active list. If you want a IWD Miami quote alongside, we are happy to provide one.

What credit score does the HEAT Loan require?

+
Typical underwriting threshold is mid-600s FICO. Some participating lenders are more flexible for income-eligible homeowners or for smaller loan amounts.

Can I pay off the HEAT Loan early?

+
Yes. No prepayment penalty. Most Florida homeowners use the Florida Energy rebate check (4–8 weeks after install) and the next-year federal tax credit refund to accelerate payoff.

Does the HEAT Loan cover a panel upgrade?

+
Yes, when the panel upgrade is required to enable an electrification scope (heat pump, EV charger, induction range). Standalone panel upgrades not tied to electrification are typically not HEAT Loan eligible.

Can I refinance the HEAT Loan into a HELOC?

+
You can pay off the HEAT Loan with HELOC funds, but at typical HELOC rates this rarely makes sense given the HEAT Loan is already 0% APR. The HEAT Loan is the cheapest financing available for Florida energy upgrades.

Does the HEAT Loan affect my home equity or property tax?

+
No. The HEAT Loan is unsecured (not a lien on the property), does not appear on your title, and has no impact on property tax assessment.

How long does HEAT Loan approval take?

+
Typical approval is 1–3 business days from application submission. IWD Miami submits on your behalf as part of the project intake.
