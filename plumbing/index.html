Why Florida Plumbing Is Different

  1. Winter is the season. Florida averages 15–25 sustained sub-zero days per winter. Older Miami-area homes — exterior wall plumbing, uninsulated basements, pre-1990 supply lines — face frozen pipe risk every January and February. Burst pipe season is a real plumbing market.
  2. Gas requires a separate license. Florida requires a Master Gas Fitter license for any gas line work — separate from the Master Plumber. We hold both. Most "plumbers" can't legally touch your gas line; we do.
  3. Pre-1978 housing stock. Most Miami housing requires EPA RRP lead-safe protocols when disturbing painted surfaces — common in pipe relocations and bathroom renovations.

How We Handle Florida Winter Pipe Emergencies

  1. Stabilize first. Shut off water at main. Document damage with photos for insurance. Locate burst point and isolate with PEX SharkBite if necessary.
  2. Master Plumber on site within 5 min. No junior tech without authority to repair. We bring full PEX, copper, fittings, soldering, propane on every winter call.
  3. Repair to code. Florida Plumbing Code (Florida Energy Code) compliant. Soldered copper or crimped PEX, properly supported, properly sloped.
  4. Reinsulate. The pipe burst because it froze. Foam insulation, electric heat tape if necessary, exterior wall sealing — fix the cause, not just the symptom.
  5. Insurance documentation. Written report with photos suitable for your insurance claim. We coordinate with your insurance company directly if you authorize.

Plumbing FAQs

Florida Plumbing Questions Answered.

Do you handle Florida climate pipe emergencies 24/7?

+
Yes. Burst pipe and frozen pipe emergencies are our peak season (January–March). 5-minute response within Greater Miami, same-day for outer Florida. Master Plumber on every emergency call — not a junior tech without repair authority.

Can you handle gas line work in Florida?

+
Yes. Florida requires a separate Master Gas Fitter license for any gas line work — we hold it in-house. Gas line repair, new gas line installation, gas appliance hookup, gas leak response, and underground PE gas main extensions all licensed.

How much does sewer line repair cost in Florida?

+
Trenchless sewer line replacement (pipe-bursting or cured-in-place lining): $5,000–$12,000 typical. Traditional dig-and-replace: $7,000–$25,000 depending on length and depth. Free camera inspection to scope the actual problem before pricing.

Do you install whole-house water filtration?

+
Yes. Whole-house carbon filtration, UV sterilization, water softeners (Watts, Culligan), and reverse osmosis under-sink systems. Common for older Florida wells and municipalities with hard water (most of Broward County and South Shore).

What sump pump systems do you install?

+
Submersible primary pumps from Zoeller and Liberty, battery backup systems for power outages, and water-powered backup pumps for high-water-table South Shore and Cape basements. Critical for Florida coastal flooding and spring melt.

Do you offer plumbing inspections for home buyers?

+
Yes. Pre-purchase plumbing inspections covering supply lines, drain lines, water heater age and condition, sump pump function, sewer lateral camera inspection, and main shut-off accessibility. Written report with photos delivered same day.

How can I prevent frozen pipes in Florida climate?

+
Insulate exterior wall plumbing. Open cabinet doors during sub-zero nights. Disconnect outdoor hoses. Maintain heat above 55°F if you travel. For chronic frozen pipe problems, we install self-regulating heat tape on vulnerable runs — pays for itself the first burst it prevents.

Do you do whole-home repiping?

+
Yes. Copper-to-PEX repiping for older Florida homes with failing copper, galvanized, or polybutylene supply lines. Typical 1,500 sq ft home: 3–5 days, $8,000–$15,000 turnkey. Coordinated with drywall repair and EPA RRP for pre-1978 homes.
