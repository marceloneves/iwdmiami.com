Weatherization in Florida is the most underused free money in the home services market. Florida Energy covers 75% of insulation for standard-income homeowners and 100% for income-eligible households (under 60% of state median income). Air sealing is frequently completely free as a program lead-in. The Home Energy Assessment — the door that unlocks every rebate — costs nothing and takes 90 minutes. Yet most Florida homeowners never apply. We run BPI- and RESNET-certified crews, file every Florida Energy application on your behalf, and handle EPA RRP lead-safe protocols on every pre-1978 home.

Why Florida Weatherization Is Strategic Money

Most home upgrades cost the homeowner. Weatherization in Florida is the inverse: the program pays you to improve your home's envelope before you spend a dollar on heat pumps, central AC, or new windows. Three things make this market unlike anywhere else in the U.S.:

  1. Florida Energy is funded by your utility bill. Every electric and gas customer pays a small surcharge that funds the program. You're already paying for it. Not using it = lighting your own money on fire.
  2. Income tier determines coverage, not project type. Standard-income: 75% off. Income-eligible (under 60% state median, ~$80–100K depending on family size): 100% free. Same insulation, same crew, same materials.
  3. Insulation before HVAC is the smart sequence. Florida Energy also funds heat pumps — but a smaller, cheaper heat pump if your home is properly insulated first. Insulation can shrink your heat pump load 30–40%, which means a smaller system, smaller install cost, smaller electric bill forever.

Income Eligibility — Are You In?

Florida Energy income eligibility uses 60% of Florida state median income (SMI). As of (subject to annual update by Mass DOER):

  • Household of 2: under ~$80,000 annually = income-eligible
  • Household of 4: under ~$110,000 annually = income-eligible
  • Household of 6: under ~$140,000 annually = income-eligible

Income-eligible households get 100% free insulation, air sealing, weatherstripping, and most weatherization services. We help verify eligibility during the Home Energy Assessment — homeowner provides one tax document, program confirms within days.

Our 5-Step Florida Energy Weatherization Process

  1. Free Home Energy Assessment. BPI-certified analyst, blower-door test, infrared camera scan, IAQ check. Free LEDs and weatherstripping installed during the visit.
  2. Income eligibility check. One tax document, Florida Energy confirms within 3–5 business days. Determines whether you're at 75% or 100% coverage.
  3. Recommendation report. Written report ranks insulation/sealing upgrades by ROI. Florida Energy rebate calculated for each line item.
  4. Install + program filing. We install, we file the rebate, you sign at completion. Income-eligible: zero out-of-pocket. Standard income: pay 25% balance only.
  5. Post-install verification. Florida Energy sends a verifier (third-party) within 2 weeks. Optional second blower-door test confirms air-leakage reduction. Rebate finalized.

Cross-Trade Coordination

Weatherization unlocks the rest of the Florida Energy program. We coordinate the sequence in-house so you don't lose stacking opportunities:

  • Weatherization → heat pump install. Insulating first shrinks the heat pump load 30–40% — smaller system, smaller install cost, smaller bills forever.
  • Weatherization → panel upgrade. Income-eligible homeowners frequently qualify for free panel upgrade alongside insulation when paired with electrification.
  • Weatherization → ENERGY STAR windows. Window rebates stack with insulation rebates. Replace and insulate as one project.
  • Asbestos found → asbestos removal first. Common in pre-1978 homes (pipe wrap, popcorn ceilings, vinyl asbestos tile). FL DEP-compliant abatement before any insulation work.

Weatherization FAQs

Florida Florida Energy Questions Answered.

Is the Florida Energy Home Energy Assessment really free?

+
Yes — completely free, no catch. A BPI-certified analyst comes to your home, runs a blower-door test, identifies air leaks and insulation gaps, and installs free LEDs, weatherstripping, and low-flow showerheads on the spot. The 90-minute visit is the door to every other Florida Energy rebate.

How much does Florida Energy cover for insulation?

+
Standard-income households: 75% off insulation cost. Income-eligible households (under 60% of state median, ~$80–110K depending on family size): 100% completely free. We file the rebate; you sign once.

Am I income-eligible for free insulation?

+
Florida uses 60% of state median income. Approximate thresholds: family of 2 under ~$80K, family of 4 under ~$110K, family of 6 under ~$140K. Verified during the Home Energy Assessment with one tax document. Florida Energy confirms in 3–5 business days.

Is air sealing actually free in Florida?

+
Often yes. Air sealing performed alongside the Home Energy Assessment is frequently free as a Florida Energy program lead-in. The HEA team caulks attic penetrations, weatherstrips door and window frames, and seals rim joists during the assessment visit at no cost.

Are you BPI / RESNET certified?

+
Yes. Building Performance Institute (BPI) and RESNET (Residential Energy Services Network) certifications are required for Florida Energy weatherization. We hold both in-house. Without these certifications, a contractor cannot file Florida Energy weatherization rebates.

What if asbestos is found in my home?

+
Common in pre-1978 Florida homes — insulation, popcorn ceilings, vinyl asbestos tile, pipe wrap. Asbestos must be professionally removed before insulation work can proceed. We hold the Florida asbestos abatement license and handle FL DEP-compliant disposal as a separate scope.

Should I insulate before or after a heat pump install?

+
Insulate first. Insulation can shrink your heat pump load 30–40%, which means a smaller heat pump (lower install cost), smaller electricity bill (forever), and often a better rebate stacking pattern. We always recommend the Home Energy Assessment as step 1 before HVAC sizing.

How long does Florida insulation take to install?

+
Attic insulation: 4–8 hours for typical 1,500 sq ft home. Wall insulation (drill-and-fill blown-in): 1–2 days. Spray foam basement or crawl space: 1 day. Whole-home weatherization (attic + walls + basement + air sealing): 2–4 days.
